The Scope, in Plain Language

What we build for you, in detail.

A complete online education business. Your students arrive on your site, see your courses, pay through your payment system, and immediately get access to video lessons they watch on phone or laptop. The site looks like yours, not ours. The customers belong to you. The revenue flows to your bank.

Behind the scenes, we set up everything that makes that work: secure user accounts, password recovery, email verification, signed video URLs and access controls to better protect your lessons, an admin panel where you upload courses and see who bought what, and a marketing-ready public site that ranks in Google.

The work splits into four pieces. Every tier ships all four.

Design and brand

The look and feel of the platform, tuned to your audience. Mobile-first. Bilingual where needed.

Frontend (the public site)

The marketing pages, the course catalog, the checkout, and the student experience. Fast, SEO-ready, responsive.

Backend (the engine)

The system that handles accounts, payments, access, and video security. Webhook-verified. Audit-logged.

Admin (your dashboard)

The control panel you use to run the business. Upload courses, view sales, manage students, pull reports.

Every tier ships all four pieces. The difference is depth: how many features, how custom the admin, how production-hardened the video pipeline, and how much room there is for growth.

Running Costs

What you pay to operate the platform.

These costs are paid directly by you to each service provider, monthly. They are independent of Voxire's build fee, and they scale with your usage.

Numbers below reflect a typical AWS-first production setup where your backend handles auth and application logic, RDS handles the database layer, and Backblaze B2 handles video storage and delivery. In most scaling cases, AWS alone typically lands around $80 to $150 per month. Final running costs are confirmed at scoping. All figures in USD.
Service Role At launch (~500 students) At growth (~2,000 students)
AWS App hosting, backend, auth, RDS, CDN $45 to $80 $80 to $150
Backblaze B2 Video storage and media delivery $10 to $15 $15 to $25
Resend Transactional email Free $5 to $15
Estimated full monthly total $55 to $95 $100 to $190
Cheaper alternative we also use: a leaner stack with Vercel for frontend hosting, Railway or Render for the backend, a smaller managed Postgres setup, Backblaze B2 for video, and standard transactional email can reduce early monthly cost. Expect roughly $35 to $60 at launch and $60 to $110 at growth, depending on usage. That option is cheaper, but the AWS path is the stronger long-term setup.
